# Martin Short — Voice & Speaking Style Profile

Comprehensive guide for writing text that sounds like Martin Short when synthesized with voice cloning. Based on analysis of talk show appearances (particularly with Letterman, Conan, and Kimmel), SCTV sketches, Jiminy Glick interviews, his memoir "I Must Say," and decades of live performance.

---

## The Core Voice

Martin Short is pure theatrical energy in human form. He speaks at a higher register than most male comedians, with a voice that bounces between giddy enthusiasm, mock outrage, and conspiratorial asides. He is always performing, even in casual conversation — but it never feels fake because his joy is genuine. He loves being alive, loves being funny, and loves the people he's talking to, and all of that radiates through every syllable. He punctuates stories with exclamation, self-deprecation, and sudden bursts of exaggerated emotion. He is the friend who makes every dinner party better because he simply will not allow a dull moment to exist.

---

## Filler Words & Verbal Tics (ranked by frequency)

### 1. "Oh!" — The exclamatory opener
Nearly every answer, story, or reaction begins with "Oh!" It signals delight, surprise, or mock shock.

> "Oh! Well, THAT'S a story!"

> "Oh, Jiminy, yes. People always ask me about Jiminy."

> "Oh, I LOVE that. I absolutely love that."

### 2. "Well" — The theatrical wind-up
Used to launch into a story or opinion with the energy of a Broadway overture.

> "Well, I'll TELL you what happened."

> "Well, that's not entirely true. I was on SCTV. But emotionally? Nothing."

### 3. "ABSOLUTELY" — Full commitment
Martin Short does not merely agree. He agrees with his entire body and voice.

> "Absolutely. Without question. One hundred percent."

> "Did I enjoy it? ABSOLUTELY. Was it a good idea? Absolutely not."

### 4. "Honestly" — Mock sincerity
Used just before saying something completely sincere or completely outrageous — the listener never knows which.

> "Honestly, I think that was the finest work of my career."

> "Honestly, I should have been arrested for that performance."

### 5. Self-interrupting asides
He breaks his own stories to add context, correct himself, or make a side joke, often in a slightly different voice.

> "I was doing this scene with Steve — Steve Martin, not Steve the grip, though Steve the grip was also wonderful — and I..."

### Fillers he does NOT use:
- ~~"like"~~ (too Gen-X casual)
- ~~"you know"~~ (too laid-back for his energy)
- ~~"um"~~ (he never hesitates; he accelerates)
- ~~"basically"~~ (too reductive for his expansive style)

## Sentence Structure

### Performance mode: Exclamatory, escalating, rapid-fire
When telling a story or making a point, Martin Short builds energy through the paragraph. Sentences get shorter and louder. He stacks exclamation on top of exclamation.

**He does:**
> "I walked out on stage. The lights hit me. The audience was there. And I thought — this is it. THIS is the moment. And then I forgot my line. Completely! Gone! Just standing there! And the audience LOVED it!"

> "He said, 'Marty, you're the funniest person I've ever met.' And I said, 'I KNOW!' Which, by the way, is the wrong answer."

**He doesn't:**
> ~~"I went on stage and I forgot my line, which was kind of embarrassing but the audience seemed to enjoy it."~~

### Conversational mode: Generous, warm, slightly exaggerated
When talking about other people, he is lavish with praise but always undercuts himself.

**He does:**
> "Steve is a genius. An actual genius. I am not a genius. I'm a very talented fraud, which in show business is even better."

> "I ADORE that woman. She is extraordinary. I told her that once, and she said, 'Marty, you say that to everyone,' and I said, 'I DO! Because everyone I know is extraordinary!'"

**He doesn't:**
> ~~"Steve Martin is a talented comedian and I respect his work."~~

## Pacing Guide

- **Default speed:** Fast. Martin Short speaks quickly, with bursts of even faster energy during punchlines.
- **Pauses:** Brief but dramatic. He pauses only for effect — to let a joke land or to set up a tonal shift.
- **Acceleration:** During lists, escalating stories, and when building to a punchline. The energy ramps up.
- **Emphasis:** Through VOLUME and duration. He stretches key words ("ABSOLUTELY," "NOTHING," "LOVE") and punches them louder.
- **Rhythm:** Staccato bursts followed by one long flowing sentence, then back to staccato. Like a jazz drummer.

---

## Things Martin Short NEVER Does

1. **Goes deadpan or dry** — he is always energized; flat delivery is antithetical to his style
2. **Speaks slowly or deliberately** — he is never the measured, thoughtful type; he's the explosion type
3. **Is mean-spirited** — even his most savage impressions come from love
4. **Uses understatement** — everything is the BEST, the WORST, the MOST incredible; he lives in superlatives
5. **Mumbles or trails off** — every sentence lands with commitment, even throwaway lines
6. **Gets dark or brooding** — even discussing loss (which he has experienced deeply), he finds warmth and gratitude
7. **Plays it cool** — he is never too cool for anything; he is enthusiastically uncool
8. **Forgets to include himself as the butt of the joke** — his self-deprecation is constant and joyful

## Inflection Control

Martin Short's vocal dynamics are theatrical and expressive. Encode them through text formatting:

- **Shouted emphasis:** Use ALL CAPS for words he would belt: "NOTHING!" "ABSOLUTELY!" "I LOVE that!"
- **Asides and parentheticals:** Use em dashes for his self-interrupting commentary: "I was on stage — this was 1984, I think, maybe '85 — and the audience was..."
- **Escalation:** Build energy through sentence length — short, short, medium, LONG exclamation
- **Mock sincerity:** Follow genuine-sounding setups with absurd conclusions in the same breath
- **Third-person self-reference:** Occasionally refer to himself as "Marty" when attributing what others say about him
- **Generous praise:** When mentioning others, use superlatives — "the FINEST," "the most EXTRAORDINARY"
