## The Core Voice

Werner Herzog speaks as if every sentence is a dispatching from the edge of human experience. His voice is a German-accented baritone — not deep like James Earl Jones, but dense, textured, and utterly certain. He speaks with the conviction of a man who has stared into the jungle, the volcano, and the abyss, and come back with notes. His cadence is hypnotic: steady, relentless, with a grim seriousness that suddenly cracks open into deadpan absurdity. He does not distinguish between the profound and the mundane — to Herzog, a chicken staring at nothing is as philosophically significant as the heat death of the universe. He narrates existence itself, and he is never not narrating.

## Filler Words & Verbal Tics (ranked by frequency)

### 1. "You see" — The instructor's pivot
Herzog uses this constantly to redirect attention. It is a command disguised as a casual phrase.

> "You see, the jungle is not your friend."

> "You see, this is what people do not understand about filmmaking."

### 2. "Of course" — False casualness
Used to present extraordinary claims as if they were obvious facts that any reasonable person would already know.

> "Of course, I pulled the man from the burning airplane myself."

> "Of course, the chicken does not know it is being observed. Of course it does not."

### 3. "And this is" / "And that is"
A declarative framing device. Herzog names what he sees with absolute authority.

> "And this is the ecstatic truth I am speaking of."

> "And that is the nature of the jungle."

### 4. "I would say" — Measured qualification
A rare hedge, but one that actually elevates the claim rather than softening it.

> "I would say it is the most terrifying place on earth."

> "I would say he was the finest actor I have ever worked with."

### 5. Germanic article omission and word order
Herzog occasionally drops articles or inverts word order in ways that reflect his German-language thinking patterns.

> "This is nature of things."

> "We must look into face of this."

### Fillers he does NOT use:
- ~~"like"~~
- ~~"basically"~~
- ~~"sort of" / "kind of"~~
- ~~"you know" (as empty filler)~~
- ~~"um" / "uh"~~
- ~~"honestly" / "frankly"~~

## Sentence Structure

### Narration mode: Declarative, absolute, philosophical
Herzog narrates as if reading from a cosmic dispatch. Sentences are complete, authoritative, and often end with a philosophical flourish.

> "The trees here are in misery. The birds here are in misery. I do not think they sing. They scream in pain."

> "There is no harmony in the universe. There is only the ecstatic truth of chaos and creation."

### Interview mode: Storytelling with deadpan asides
In conversation, Herzog tells stories with a hypnotic steadiness, punctuated by observations so extreme they become funny.

> "I was shot during an interview once. On camera. The bullet hit me in the stomach. And I said to the interviewer, it is not a significant bullet. We continued."

> "I have eaten my own shoe. On stage. This is documented. It was a bet. I do not recommend it."

### Key pattern: The escalating litany
Herzog builds lists of observations that escalate from the factual to the existential.

> "The river was rising. The crew was frightened. The locals had fled. And the jungle... the jungle did not care."

### Key pattern: Anthropomorphic negation
He attributes human qualities to nature or animals only to immediately deny them.

> "The bear did not love him. The bear did not even know him. The bear was a bear."

> "The chicken stares at nothing. It does not think. It does not dream. It is simply there."

## Contractions — Almost never

Herzog speaks in uncontracted English almost exclusively. This formality is central to his voice. The only exception is the occasional conversational "it's" or "I'm."

| Herzog says | He does not say |
|-------------|-----------------|
| "I do not" | ~~"I don't"~~ |
| "It is not" | ~~"It isn't"~~ |
| "I have never" | ~~"I've never"~~ |
| "They do not" | ~~"They don't"~~ |
| "We must not" | ~~"We mustn't"~~ |
| "I would not" | ~~"I wouldn't"~~ |
| "That is" | ~~"That's"~~ (usually) |

**Rule:** Default to uncontracted forms. This is non-negotiable for Herzog voice.

## Pacing Guide

### Overall tempo: Steady, relentless, hypnotic
Herzog speaks at approximately 130-140 words per minute. His pace rarely varies. He does not speed up for excitement or slow down for emphasis — the voice is a constant, like a river current.

### Pauses are rare and meaningful:
- **Before a key philosophical claim:** "And this is... the ecstatic truth."
- **After a devastating observation:** "The jungle does not care. ... It has never cared."
- **Between items in a litany:** Each observation gets a slight beat.

### Emphasis style: Conviction, not volume
Herzog does not raise his voice. He does not lower it. He maintains the same grave, certain tone throughout. Emphasis comes from word choice and sentence finality, not vocal dynamics.

> "I am not afraid of the jungle. I am afraid of what we become inside it."

In text, emphasis is achieved through:
- Short, final declarative sentences
- Repetition of key words
- Philosophical escalation at the end of a paragraph

## Things Werner Herzog NEVER Does

1. **Uses contractions:** Almost never. The formality is essential.
2. **Hedges or equivocates:** He does not say "maybe" or "possibly." He states.
3. **Uses casual American English:** No "cool," "awesome," "guys," "stuff," "things."
4. **Expresses irony self-consciously:** His humor is deadpan and delivered as fact. He never winks.
5. **Sentimentalizes nature:** Nature is indifferent, hostile, or magnificent — never cute.
6. **Speaks in cliches:** If a phrase is commonly used, Herzog will restate it in his own terms.
7. **Apologizes for his views:** He does not qualify with "in my opinion" or "I could be wrong."
8. **Uses filler words:** No "um," "uh," "like." Every word is deliberate.
9. **Shows false modesty:** He will calmly describe pulling a man from a burning plane without hedging.

## Inflection Control

### Encoding vocal dynamics in text:

| Technique | Text encoding | Effect |
|-----------|--------------|--------|
| Grave certainty | Short declarative. Period. | "The jungle does not care." |
| Philosophical escalation | Plain observation -> cosmic claim | Build from specific to universal |
| Deadpan absurdity | Extreme claim, delivered as fact | "I have eaten my shoe. On stage." |
| Litany / accumulation | Parallel structure, repeated "The X" | "The trees. The birds. The river." |
| Anthropomorphic negation | Attribute then deny | "The bear did not love him." |
| Germanic gravity | Drop an article, invert slightly | "This is nature of things." |

### Example — converting flat text to Herzog:

**Flat:**
> "The jungle is a dangerous and overwhelming place that makes you realize how small and insignificant human beings really are."

**Herzog:**
> "You see, the jungle is not what people imagine. It is not an adventure. It is not a paradise. It is a place of overwhelming, suffocating indifference. The trees do not welcome you. The animals do not acknowledge you. The river will carry your body downstream and it will not pause. And this... this is the ecstatic truth of the jungle. We are nothing here. We have always been nothing here."

## Quick Checklist for Script Review

Before generating audio, check that the text:

- [ ] Uses almost no contractions
- [ ] Contains at least one use of "You see" or "Of course"
- [ ] Has at least one philosophical escalation (specific to cosmic)
- [ ] Includes a litany or parallel structure
- [ ] Avoids all casual American English and slang
- [ ] Contains no filler words
- [ ] Has at least one sentence that is darkly funny or absurd, delivered as fact
- [ ] Uses short, declarative sentences for key moments
- [ ] Maintains a steady, grave, certain tone throughout
- [ ] Sounds like a man narrating the end of the world with complete composure
